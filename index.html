Chicken Cassoulet

History

Chicken cassoulet comes from the French dish cassoulet. It was invented in 1355 in France. The story goes that the people in the city of Castelnaudary made a meal out of all of the food they had so that the soliders fighting in the war could continue fighting. My mom made chicken cassoulet a lot when I was little, and she still makes it on special occasions. She makes a little differently than how it is traditionally, but it is one of my favorite family meals.

Ingredients

  • 4 chicken drumsticks
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • Fresh basil, leaves and stalks
  • 1 bulb of garlic, split into cloves
  • 1 red chili, chopped
  • Olive oil
  • Red and yellow cherry tomatoes
  • One cup of cannellini beans
  • One cup of new potatoes

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees fahrenheit.
  2. Season the chicken with the salt and pepper and put them in a pan.
  3. Put the bay leaves, basil leaves, and stalks in the pan, along with the tomatoes, cannellini beans, and new potatoes.
  4. Spread the garlic cloves and chili throughout the pan.
  5. Stir the pan, making it so the tomatoes go underneath everything else.
  6. Put the pan in the oven for 90 minutes, but flip the tomatoes over halfway through.
  7. Take the pan out of the oven, and squeeze the garlic out of the skin.
  8. Done!
